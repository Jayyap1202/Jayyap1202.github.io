Place To Visit

Located inside the state of Terengganu you will find beautiful little villages and towns, as well as idyllic islands and of course the star attraction which is the capital of Kuala Terengganu itself.

CHINA TOWN

Kuala Terengganu is blessed with a large Chinese population and with that in mind you can visit the pretty area of Chinatown when you are in the city. Here you will find many traditional shops and houses that are built in the Chinese style and you can wander around the winding streets here and enjoy the sights.

PULAU REDANG

Redang Island or Redang Island is part of the wider Redang Island Marine Park, a part of Malaysia that is known for having an array of underwater fun. With 500 different types of coral and more than 3,000 species of fish, snorkeling and diving are popular pastimes here.

CRYSTAL MOSQUE

The Crystal Mosque is known as Masjid Kristal in Malay and is said to be one of the prettiest Muslim places of worship in all of Malaysia.If possible try to also visit the mosque at night as it is illuminated in a riot of colors such as pink, yellow, blue and green which make it sparkle under the dark skies.

FLOATING FERRY TERMINAL

This floating ferry terminal (approx. 5,000 m²) is situated in the largest reservoir of South-East Asia. Tasik Kenyir (or Kenyir Lake) spans over 260km2, with about 340 small islands, 14 waterfalls and many rapids.

DRAWBRIDGE KT

Kuala Terengganu Drawbridge or Jambatan Angkat Kuala Terengganu is a bascule bridge in Kuala Terengganu, Terengganu, Malaysia, which crosses the mouth of Terengganu River. The bridge is the fourth bridge in Kuala Terengganu City area that straddles the river after Sultan Mahmud Bridge, Manir Bridge, and Pulau Sekati Bridge.

FLOATING MOSQUE

The Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque or the Floating Mosque is the first real floating mosque in Malaysia. The mosque combines modern and Moorish architecture; incorporating the use of marble, ceramics, mosaic works and bomanite paving.
